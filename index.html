MOTHER LAND

"THE PHOTO WHICH YOUR ARE SEEING IN THE BACKGROUNG IS MY MOTHERLAND" With Refrence That Mother And Motherland Are Greater Than Heaven I've always had a strong connection to my motherland, particularly to the village where I was born and raised. It's a small community nestled in the countryside, surrounded by lush green fields and rolling hills. As a child, I spent countless hours playing in those fields, picking wildflowers, and chasing after farm animals. The village is where my grandparents and extended family still reside, and it's where I've formed some of my closest friendships and fondest memories. Whenever I return to my motherland and step foot in the village, it feels like coming home. The people, the sights, the sounds, and the smells all fill me with a sense of comfort and familiarity. I'm grateful for the village's influence on my life and the role it's played in shaping my identity and cultural heritage.

Who Am I?

Hi, my name is Grish Khadka, and I'm currently a student in Class 11 at St. Xavier's School Jawalakhel. I'm originally from Nepal and spent most of my childhood studying at Nepal Armed Police Force School. I'm now 17 years old, and I'm grateful for the experiences and education that have brought me to where I am today. Being a part of the Nepal Armed Police Force School community for seven years has taught me valuable life skills, such as discipline, hard work, and dedication, which I hope to apply to my current studies and future endeavors. I'm excited to see what the future holds and look forward to continuing my education and personal growth at St. Xavier's School Jawalakhel.
